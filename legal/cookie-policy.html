4. Session vs. Persistent Cookies

4.1. Session Cookies (Temporary):

These are deleted when you close your browser. They keep you logged in during your visit.

Example: session_id, load_balancer

4.2. Persistent Cookies (Long-term):

These remain on your device for a set period (days, months, or years) even after you close the browser.

Example: user_lang (1 year), _ga (2 years)

6. How to Control Cookies

You have full control over cookies. Here's how to manage them:

Option 1: Cookie Settings on Our Website (Recommended)

Click the "Cookie Preferences" button at the bottom of any page to:

  • View all cookies we use
  • Enable/disable functional cookies
  • Enable/disable analytics cookies
  • View cookie details (name, purpose, expiry)

Note: Strictly necessary cookies cannot be disabled as they're required for the site to function.

Option 2: Browser Settings

You can block or delete cookies directly in your browser settings:

Google Chrome:

Settings ‚Üí Privacy and security ‚Üí Cookies and other site data

Firefox:

Settings ‚Üí Privacy & Security ‚Üí Cookies and Site Data

Safari:

Preferences ‚Üí Privacy ‚Üí Manage Website Data

Microsoft Edge:

Settings ‚Üí Cookies and site permissions ‚Üí Cookies and site data

‚ö†Ô∏è Warning: Blocking all cookies may prevent you from logging in or using certain features.

Option 3: Opt-Out of Google Analytics

Install Google's official opt-out browser add-on:

https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout

This prevents Google Analytics from collecting data about your visits to all websites, not just ours.

Option 4: Do Not Track (DNT)

Most browsers allow you to enable "Do Not Track" (DNT) signals. However, there is no universal standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals.

Our Approach: We honor DNT signals for analytics cookies. If DNT is enabled, we will not load Google Analytics.
