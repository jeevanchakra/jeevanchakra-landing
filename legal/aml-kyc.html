Anti-Money Laundering & Know Your Customer Policy

Last Updated: January 14, 2025

Jeevan Chakra is committed to preventing money laundering, terrorist financing, and financial crimes. This Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Know Your Customer (KYC) Policy outlines our procedures to comply with Indian laws and international standards.

1. Legal & Regulatory Framework

This policy is based on:

1.1. Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA), 2002 and PMLA Rules

1.2. RBI Master Direction on KYC (updated periodically)

1.3. SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013

1.4. Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967 (UAPA)

1.5. Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), 1999

1.6. Financial Action Task Force (FATF) Recommendations

1.7. Income Tax Act, 1961 (PAN requirements)

2. Who Must Comply

This policy applies to:

2.1. All clients and users of Jeevan Chakra services

2.2. Indian residents, NRIs, and foreign nationals investing in India

2.3. Both individual and corporate/institutional clients

2.4. All Jeevan Chakra employees, agents, and representatives

‚ö†Ô∏è Mandatory Compliance: You CANNOT use our services without completing KYC. Providing false KYC information is a criminal offense under Indian law.

3. KYC Documentation Requirements

You MUST provide the following documents:

A. For Individual Indian Residents:

Mandatory Documents:

  • PAN Card (Permanent Account Number) - Mandatory for all financial transactions above ‚Çπ50,000
  • Aadhaar Card (with consent for authentication) OR alternative ID proof:
    • Passport
    • Voter ID
    • Driving License
  • Proof of Address (if different from ID):
    • Bank statement (last 3 months)
    • Utility bill (electricity, water, gas)
    • Rent agreement
    • Aadhaar (if not used as primary ID)
  • Recent Photograph (passport size)
  • Bank Account Proof: Cancelled cheque or bank statement

Documents must be valid, clear, and not older than 3 months (for address proof).

B. For Non-Resident Indians (NRIs):

  • Valid Indian Passport
  • PAN Card (mandatory)
  • Overseas Address Proof:
    • Foreign utility bill, bank statement, or residence permit
    • Must show current country of residence
  • NRE/NRO Bank Account details (for repatriation compliance)
  • Photograph
  • Tax Residency Certificate (if claiming tax treaty benefits)

C. For Foreign Nationals (Non-Indians):

  • Valid Passport with valid Indian visa (if required)
  • PAN Card (issued by Indian Income Tax Department) - Mandatory
  • Proof of Overseas Address
  • Photograph
  • Source of Funds Declaration
  • FEMA Compliance: Declaration of investment under FDI/FII/FVCI route (if applicable)

Note: Foreign portfolio investments in India are subject to FEMA regulations and require RBI/SEBI approvals.

D. Additional Requirements for High-Value Clients:

For investments above ‚Çπ10 Lakhs or classified as "High Risk," we may require:

  • Income Proof: ITR, Form 16, salary slips
  • Net Worth Statement
  • Source of Funds Declaration (detailed explanation of fund origins)
  • Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD): Additional identity verification, in-person verification, or video KYC

4. KYC Verification Process

Our verification process:

4.1. Document Submission: Upload documents through our secure platform.

4.2. Automated Verification: Documents are verified using:

  • PAN verification with Income Tax database
  • Aadhaar authentication (with your consent via OTP)
  • Bank account verification (penny drop test)

4.3. Manual Review: Our compliance team reviews all documents for authenticity.

4.4. Video KYC (if required): Live video verification as per RBI guidelines.

4.5. CKYC Registration: Your KYC is registered with Central KYC Registry (CERSAI) as per RBI mandate.

4.6. Approval Timeline: KYC verification typically completed within 2-5 business days.

Rejection Reasons: KYC may be rejected if documents are:

  • Expired, illegible, or tampered
  • Do not match across documents (name/address mismatch)
  • Belong to someone else (identity theft attempt)
  • Suspected to be fraudulent

5. Ongoing KYC & Transaction Monitoring

5.1. Periodic KYC Updates: You must update KYC every 10 years (as per RBI) or whenever there are material changes (address, name, etc.).

5.2. Transaction Monitoring: We monitor all transactions for suspicious patterns:

  • Unusually large or frequent transactions
  • Transactions inconsistent with your profile
  • Sudden changes in investment behavior
  • Attempts to avoid reporting thresholds

5.3. Source of Funds Verification: We may request additional documentation if:

  • Investment amount is significantly higher than declared income
  • Funds come from high-risk jurisdictions
  • Multiple transactions from different bank accounts

6. Risk-Based Customer Classification

We classify customers into risk categories:

Low Risk Customers:

  • Salaried employees with stable income
  • Government employees, PSU employees
  • Small investments (under ‚Çπ5 Lakhs/year)
  • Fully verified KYC with CKYC registration

Requirements: Standard KYC documentation.

Medium Risk Customers:

  • Self-employed professionals, business owners
  • NRIs from low-risk countries
  • Medium investments (‚Çπ5-25 Lakhs/year)

Requirements: Standard KYC + Income proof.

High Risk Customers (Enhanced Due Diligence):

  • Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) - government officials, public figures
  • NRIs from high-risk jurisdictions (FATF blacklist/grey list countries)
  • Cash-intensive businesses (real estate, jewelry, gaming)
  • High-value investments (above ‚Çπ25 Lakhs/year)
  • Clients with complex ownership structures
  • Clients refusing to provide source of funds

Requirements: Enhanced KYC + Source of wealth documentation + Senior management approval.

7. Suspicious Transaction Reporting

We are legally required to report suspicious activities to authorities. Examples include:

üö® Red Flags We Monitor:

  • Structuring transactions to avoid reporting thresholds (e.g., multiple deposits of ‚Çπ9.9 Lakhs)
  • Providing false or inconsistent information
  • Refusing to provide source of funds or KYC documents
  • Transactions with sanctioned countries or entities
  • Frequent deposits and immediate withdrawals (layering)
  • Use of multiple bank accounts without clear purpose
  • Transactions inconsistent with customer profile (e.g., low-income individual making high-value investments)
  • Reluctance to meet in person or provide contact information
  • Use of shell companies or nominees to hide beneficial ownership

Reporting Obligations:

7.1. To FIU-IND: Suspicious Transaction Reports (STR) filed with Financial Intelligence Unit - India within 7 days of detection.

7.2. Cash Transactions: All cash transactions above ‚Çπ10 Lakhs reported to FIU-IND (CTR - Cash Transaction Report).

7.3. Confidentiality: We CANNOT inform you if we file an STR (as per PMLA guidelines).

8. Prohibited & Restricted Customers

WE WILL NOT ONBOARD OR SERVE:

  • Individuals/entities on UN sanctions lists, OFAC lists, or Indian government watch lists
  • Persons from FATF high-risk jurisdictions (Iran, North Korea, etc.) unless approved exceptions
  • Shell companies with no genuine business activity
  • Customers refusing to complete KYC
  • Individuals providing false or forged documents
  • Entities involved in illegal activities (terrorism financing, drug trafficking, arms dealing)
  • Anonymous or pseudonymous accounts (cryptocurrency wallets without identity)

9. Record Retention

As required by PMLA and RBI guidelines:

9.1. KYC Documents: Retained for 7 years after account closure.

9.2. Transaction Records: All investment transactions retained for 7 years.

9.3. Communication Records: Emails, calls, chats retained for 7 years.

9.4. STR/CTR Reports: Copies retained for 7 years.

9.5. Format: Records maintained in both physical and electronic form, securely encrypted.

10. Internal Controls & Training

10.1. Compliance Officer: Designated AML-KYC Compliance Officer responsible for policy implementation.

10.2. Employee Training: All staff receive annual AML-KYC training.

10.3. Independent Audit: Annual AML audit by external auditor.

10.4. Technology: Automated transaction monitoring systems to detect suspicious patterns.

11. Your Responsibilities

You MUST:

11.1. Provide accurate, complete, and genuine KYC documents.

11.2. Update KYC information whenever there are material changes (address, name, citizenship).

11.3. Respond promptly to requests for additional information or clarification.

11.4. Not use our services for money laundering, terrorism financing, or any illegal activities.

11.5. Ensure funds invested are from legitimate sources.

Legal Consequences: Providing false KYC information or using our services for illegal activities is punishable under PMLA (imprisonment up to 7 years and fines) and other Indian laws.

12. Account Suspension or Termination

We reserve the right to immediately suspend or terminate your account if:

  • KYC documents are found to be false, forged, or expired
  • You refuse to update KYC or provide additional documentation
  • Suspicious activity is detected
  • You appear on sanctions lists or are classified as high-risk without adequate documentation
  • Required by law enforcement or regulatory authorities

Impact: Account freezing, investment redemption delays, reporting to FIU-IND.

13. Cooperation with Law Enforcement

13.1. We fully cooperate with Indian law enforcement, FIU-IND, Enforcement Directorate (ED), CBI, and other authorities.

13.2. We may be required to freeze accounts or provide customer information without prior notice to you.

13.3. We comply with all legal summons, court orders, and regulatory inquiries.
