2. Investment Risks You Must Understand

A. Market Risk

Securities prices fluctuate based on market conditions, economic factors, and investor sentiment. Your investments may lose value due to market downturns, recessions, or economic crises.

B. Credit/Default Risk

Bonds, P2P lending, and debt instruments carry risk that the issuer may default on payments. You could lose both interest income and principal if the borrower/issuer fails to repay.

C. Interest Rate Risk

Bond prices fall when interest rates rise. Fixed-income investments may lose market value if rates increase, even if the issuer doesn't default.

D. Liquidity Risk

Some investments (bonds, InvITs, REITs, P2P) may not be easily sellable at desired prices. You may face delays or losses when trying to exit positions.

E. Inflation Risk

Your returns may not keep pace with inflation, resulting in reduced purchasing power over time, even if nominal returns are positive.

F. Regulatory/Political Risk

Government policies, tax law changes, or regulatory actions may adversely affect investment performance or your ability to access funds.

G. Currency Risk (for NRIs)

If you're investing from abroad, exchange rate fluctuations may impact your returns when converted to your home currency.
